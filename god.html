  Ganesh 

        Ganesha getting ready to throw his lotus. Basohli miniature, circa 1730. National Museum, New Delhi.
        In the Mudgalapurāṇa (VII, 70), in order to kill the demon of egotism (Mamāsura) who had attacked him,
        Gaṇeśa Vighnarāja throws his lotus at him. Unable to bear the fragrance of the divine flower, the demon surrenders to Gaṇeśha
        
      

       
       
  Shiva 

       Shiva , also known as Mahadeva (/ˈməhɑː dɛvə/; Sanskrit: महादेव:, romanized: Mahādevaḥ, lit. 'The Great God' [mɐɦaːd̪eːʋɐ]), 
       is one of the principal deities of Hinduism. Lord Shiva is said to be the deity of death and time.
        In Sanskrit language, Kal means time. He is the Supreme Being in Shaivism, one of the major traditions within Hinduism.

      
         

       
  Vishnu 
   
        Vishnu , also known as Narayana and Hari, is one of the principal deities of Hinduism.
        He is the supreme being within Vaishnavism, one of the major traditions within contemporary Hinduism. 
         Vishnu is known as "The Preserver" within the Trimurti

      


       
  brahma 
  
         Brahma (Sanskrit: ब्रह्मा, romanized: Brahmā) is referred to as "The Creator" within the Trimurti, 
         the triple deity of supreme divinity that includes Vishnu, and Shiva.
         He is also referred to as Svayambhu (lit. 'self-born') and is associated with creation, knowledge and Vedas.
